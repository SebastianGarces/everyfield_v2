Checklists transform well-intentioned volunteers into effective ministry teams. They ensure attention to detail and consistency in execution week after week.

While these checklists are prepared for Launch Sunday, implement them every week. What follows are starting points—customize based on your facility, team structure, and ministry approach.

---

## Set-up and Tear-down Team

The set-up team is often the unsung hero of Sunday morning. Their work creates the environment where ministry happens.

### Pre-Service Setup (2+ hours before service)

- [ ] Arrive on time with vehicle keys and facility access
- [ ] Unload trailer/storage in correct staging areas
- [ ] Set up staging and backdrop
- [ ] Position and connect all sound equipment
- [ ] Set up projection screen(s) and projector(s)
- [ ] Position and focus lighting
- [ ] Arrange seating according to layout plan
- [ ] Place welcome center table and materials
- [ ] Set up coffee/hospitality station
- [ ] Position internal signage
- [ ] Complete sound check with worship team
- [ ] Walk through complete setup for any issues

### Post-Service Tear-down

- [ ] Begin tear-down after final guest departs
- [ ] Follow reverse order of setup
- [ ] Return all items to proper storage locations
- [ ] Check all areas for left items
- [ ] Secure facility according to agreement
- [ ] Confirm all equipment is accounted for
- [ ] Report any damaged or missing items

<Callout type="tip">
  Take photos of your ideal setup. New volunteers can reference them to 
  understand exactly how things should look.
</Callout>

## Signage and Parking Team

First impressions happen in the parking lot. This team creates a welcoming experience from the moment guests arrive.

### Exterior Setup (90 minutes before service)

- [ ] Place parking lot A-frame signs
- [ ] Position entrance banner
- [ ] Set up directional signs to building entrance
- [ ] Confirm accessible parking is clearly marked
- [ ] Test any electronic signs or displays
- [ ] Position parking team volunteers at key locations

### During Service

- [ ] Greet guests as they arrive
- [ ] Direct traffic flow during peak arrival
- [ ] Assist with parking as needed
- [ ] Monitor lot for any issues
- [ ] Help guests locate the building entrance
- [ ] Provide umbrella assistance in inclement weather

### Post-Service

- [ ] Assist with safe traffic exit
- [ ] Collect all exterior signage
- [ ] Store signs properly for next week
- [ ] Report any signage damage or loss

---

## Facility Team

This team ensures the physical space is welcoming, safe, and functional.

### Pre-Service (2 hours before)

- [ ] Unlock all necessary doors
- [ ] Turn on HVAC and adjust to comfortable temperature
- [ ] Turn on all interior lights
- [ ] Check all restrooms for cleanliness and supplies
- [ ] Walk through all guest areas for cleanliness
- [ ] Remove any hazards or obstacles
- [ ] Confirm emergency exits are clear and marked
- [ ] Set out any needed facility supplies

### During Service

- [ ] Monitor temperature and adjust as needed
- [ ] Restock restroom supplies as needed
- [ ] Address any facility issues that arise
- [ ] Keep lobby and common areas tidy

### Post-Service

- [ ] Walk through all spaces used
- [ ] Return facility to pre-service condition
- [ ] Turn off lights and HVAC per agreement
- [ ] Lock all doors
- [ ] Report any facility issues to facility owner

---

## Greeters and Ushers

Greeters and ushers create the relational connection that makes guests feel welcome.

### Greeter Checklist

**Pre-Service (30 minutes before)**

- [ ] Arrive early and check in with team lead
- [ ] Review any special instructions for the day
- [ ] Put on name tag
- [ ] Position at assigned location
- [ ] Ensure doors are propped open (if appropriate)
- [ ] Have bulletins/connection cards ready to distribute

**During Arrival Window**

- [ ] Smile and make eye contact
- [ ] Open doors for arriving guests
- [ ] Offer a warm verbal greeting
- [ ] Direct guests to children's check-in, restrooms, seating
- [ ] Note any guests who seem lost or uncertain
- [ ] Hand out bulletins or connection cards

### Usher Checklist

**Pre-Service**

- [ ] Confirm seating layout is correct
- [ ] Check for any items left in seats
- [ ] Prepare offering containers
- [ ] Review any special seating instructions

**During Service**

- [ ] Help late arrivals find seats
- [ ] Monitor for guests needing assistance
- [ ] Prepare for offering collection
- [ ] Collect offering and secure immediately
- [ ] Assist with any disruptions gracefully
- [ ] Help maintain orderly dismissal

## Children's Ministry Team

Children's ministry requires the most detailed checklists due to safety and security requirements.

### Room Setup (90 minutes before service)

**All Rooms**
- [ ] Unlock and enter room
- [ ] Turn on lights and HVAC
- [ ] Inspect for cleanliness and safety hazards
- [ ] Set up check-in station
- [ ] Verify check-in system is operational
- [ ] Prepare name tags/labels
- [ ] Set out curriculum materials
- [ ] Prepare snacks (if applicable)
- [ ] Set out age-appropriate toys/activities
- [ ] Confirm all supplies are stocked

**Infant/Toddler Rooms**
- [ ] Sanitize all toys and surfaces
- [ ] Check diaper supply and wipes
- [ ] Prepare bottles/feeding area
- [ ] Set out cribs/rest mats

**Preschool/Elementary**
- [ ] Arrange seating for lesson time
- [ ] Set up any needed AV equipment
- [ ] Prepare craft supplies
- [ ] Set out activity stations

### Check-in Procedures

- [ ] Greet parents warmly
- [ ] Verify family information in system
- [ ] Issue matching security tags to parent and child
- [ ] Collect any special instructions (allergies, needs)
- [ ] Walk child to appropriate classroom
- [ ] Introduce child to classroom teacher

### During Service

- [ ] Maintain adult-to-child ratios
- [ ] Never leave children unattended
- [ ] Follow bathroom procedures (two-adult rule)
- [ ] Document any incidents or concerns
- [ ] Contact parents if needed via security tag system

### Checkout Procedures

- [ ] Match security tags before releasing child
- [ ] Never release child without matching tag
- [ ] Communicate any relevant information to parents
- [ ] Collect child's belongings and crafts
- [ ] Warmly dismiss family

<Callout type="warning">
  Never compromise on security procedures. Every child must be checked in and 
  out properly, with matching security tags, every single week.
</Callout>

## Worship and Production Team

This team leads the congregation in worship and manages all technical production.

### Pre-Service (90 minutes before)

**Tech/Production**
- [ ] Power on all equipment
- [ ] Test projector(s) and screens
- [ ] Load presentation slides and verify content
- [ ] Test all microphones
- [ ] Conduct full sound check with worship team
- [ ] Set lighting levels for worship and message
- [ ] Test any video content
- [ ] Verify backup systems are ready

**Worship Team**
- [ ] Arrive for sound check
- [ ] Review set list and any changes
- [ ] Practice transitions
- [ ] Pray together as a team
- [ ] Final tune and preparation

### During Service

- [ ] Execute lighting cues
- [ ] Advance slides on cue
- [ ] Monitor sound levels and adjust as needed
- [ ] Record service (if applicable)
- [ ] Manage any video elements
- [ ] Respond to any technical issues calmly

### Post-Service

- [ ] Power down all equipment properly
- [ ] Store microphones and instruments
- [ ] Save and backup recordings
- [ ] Report any equipment issues
- [ ] Pack and store all gear

---

## Assimilation Team

This team captures guest information and initiates the follow-up process.

### Pre-Service

- [ ] Set up welcome center/guest table
- [ ] Prepare connection cards and pens
- [ ] Set out guest gifts (if applicable)
- [ ] Ensure friendship registers are in seats
- [ ] Test any digital check-in systems
- [ ] Brief team on follow-up procedures

### During and After Service

- [ ] Staff welcome center throughout service
- [ ] Greet guests who approach with warmth
- [ ] Answer questions about the church
- [ ] Collect completed connection cards
- [ ] Ensure all friendship registers are collected
- [ ] Enter guest data same day (within hours if possible)
- [ ] Prepare list for follow-up team

### Guest Follow-up Launch

- [ ] Compile guest list with contact information
- [ ] Send to Senior Pastor for follow-up calls
- [ ] Prepare for 48-hour follow-up process
- [ ] Update database with all new contacts

---

## Using These Checklists

### Before Launch Sunday

1. Customize each checklist for your context
2. Assign ownership to team leaders
3. Print physical copies for Launch Sunday
4. Train teams on checklist usage

### On Launch Sunday

1. Distribute checklists to team leads
2. Use checklists to verify completion
3. Note any items that need adjustment
4. Collect checklists after service for review

### After Launch Sunday

1. Debrief with team leads
2. Update checklists based on experience
3. Add any missed items
4. Remove any irrelevant items
5. Prepare updated versions for next Sunday
