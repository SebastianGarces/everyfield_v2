Effective guest follow-up and assimilation requires more than good intentions—it requires good systems. The right software helps you track every guest, manage follow-up tasks, and move people from first-time visitor to engaged member.

This guide covers what to look for in assimilation and church management software.

---

## Why Software Matters for Assimilation

Without a system, things fall through the cracks. Software helps you:

**Never lose a guest** — Every visitor is captured and tracked.

**Follow up consistently** — Automated reminders ensure timely contact.

**See the big picture** — Reports show patterns and progress.

**Coordinate teams** — Multiple people can access and update information.

**Scale effectively** — Systems that work for 50 guests work for 500.

<Callout type="insight">
  The goal isn't software proficiency—it's transformed lives. Software is a tool that frees you to focus on people by handling the administrative details.
</Callout>

---

## Key Features to Evaluate

When comparing options, look for these capabilities.

### Database Management

The foundation of any church management system is its database:

- **Contact management** — Store and organize information about people
- **Household linking** — Connect family members together
- **Custom fields** — Track information specific to your church
- **Search and filter** — Find people easily
- **Data import/export** — Move data in and out as needed

### Guest Tracking

Specifically for assimilation, you need:

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| First-time guest identification | Trigger appropriate follow-up |
| Visit tracking | Know when and how often someone attends |
| Guest journey stages | Track where each person is in the assimilation process |
| Automated workflows | Trigger follow-up tasks and communications |
| Source tracking | Know how guests heard about you |

### Follow-Up Management

- **Task assignment** — Assign follow-up calls, visits, or emails to team members
- **Reminders** — Alert team members when follow-up is due
- **Communication logging** — Record what happened in each interaction
- **Progress tracking** — See which guests have been contacted

### Check-In Integration

Many church management platforms include check-in capabilities:

- First-time guest registration at check-in
- Automatic database entry
- Family check-in for children's ministry
- Attendance tracking

### Accounting Integration

Your assimilation software may connect to financial systems:

- Contribution tracking
- Donor management
- Financial reporting
- Integration with giving platforms

## Popular Options

Several platforms serve churches well. Here are common choices:

### All-in-One Church Management Systems

**Planning Center** — Popular, modular approach. Strong in services planning, check-in, and people management.

**Church Community Builder (CCB)** — Comprehensive database with groups and assimilation features.

**Fellowship One** — Full-featured platform with strong database and accounting integration.

**Breeze** — Simpler, more affordable option good for smaller churches.

**Elvanto** — International option with good feature set.

### Specialized Tools

Some churches use specialized tools for specific needs:

- **Mailchimp/Constant Contact** — Email marketing
- **Asana/Monday.com** — Task management
- **Google Workspace** — Basic contact management via Google Contacts

<Callout type="tip">
  For most church plants, an all-in-one church management system is the best choice. Integration between modules saves time and reduces errors compared to cobbling together separate tools.
</Callout>

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Define Your Requirements

Before looking at options, clarify what you need:

- What processes need to be supported?
- Who will use the system?
- What's your budget?
- What other systems need to integrate?
- What's your technical comfort level?

### Step 2: Research Options

- Read reviews from churches similar to yours
- Ask your coach and partner churches what they use
- Check vendor websites for feature lists and pricing

### Step 3: Request Demos

Most platforms offer demonstrations:

- Schedule demos of 2-3 top candidates
- Bring your specific questions
- Ask to see features relevant to your needs
- Evaluate ease of use for your team

### Step 4: Trial Period

Many platforms offer free trials:

- Enter real (or realistic) data
- Test key workflows
- Involve team members who will use the system
- Evaluate support responsiveness

### Step 5: Make a Decision

Consider:

- Feature fit with your needs
- Ease of use
- Total cost
- Support quality
- Scalability for growth

## Implementation Considerations

### Data Entry

Getting your existing contacts into the system:

- Clean your data before importing
- Map fields correctly
- Verify data after import
- Plan for ongoing data hygiene

### Training

Your team needs to know how to use the system:

- Initial training for all users
- Role-specific training for key functions
- Documentation for reference
- Ongoing refreshers as needed

### Workflow Setup

Configure the system to match your processes:

- Define guest journey stages
- Set up automated workflows
- Create task templates
- Establish notification preferences

<Callout type="warning">
  Don't just implement software—implement processes. The software should support well-defined workflows, not replace the need to think through your assimilation strategy.
</Callout>

## Integration with Assimilation Strategy

Your software should support your assimilation strategy, not define it.

### Typical Assimilation Stages

| Stage | What Happens | Software Support |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| Guest | First visit | Record contact, trigger welcome workflow |
| Returning Guest | Subsequent visits | Track attendance, continue follow-up |
| Regular Attender | Consistent attendance | Move to connection opportunities |
| Connected | In a small group or serving | Track involvement, celebrate progress |
| Member | Formal commitment | Update status, continue development |

### Workflow Automation

Set up automated actions:

- When a new guest is entered, assign a follow-up task
- Send a welcome email after first visit
- Alert assimilation team when someone visits 3+ times
- Notify small group leader when someone is assigned

## Budget Considerations

Church management software costs vary widely:

| Platform Type | Typical Monthly Cost |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Basic/simple | $20-50/month |
| Full-featured | $50-150/month |
| Enterprise | $150-500+/month |

### Cost Factors

- Number of users
- Database size
- Features included
- Support level
- Add-on modules

### Budget Tips

- Start with essential features, add modules later
- Look for church plant discounts
- Consider annual payment discounts
- Factor in training time and costs

## Making the Transition

If you're moving from another system (or no system):

### Migration Planning

- Export all data from current system
- Map data fields to new system
- Clean data before importing
- Plan for parallel operation during transition

### Transition Timeline

1. Select new platform
2. Configure and customize
3. Train team
4. Migrate data
5. Run parallel systems briefly
6. Cut over to new system
7. Retire old system

### Common Challenges

- Data quality issues during migration
- Team resistance to new workflows
- Feature gaps between systems
- Learning curve during transition

## Success Metrics

How do you know if your software is working?

### Metrics to Track

- Guest follow-up completion rate
- Time from first visit to first follow-up
- Return visitor percentage
- Progression through assimilation stages
- Team task completion rates

### Regular Review

- Weekly: Check task completion, outstanding follow-ups
- Monthly: Review metrics, identify bottlenecks
- Quarterly: Evaluate system effectiveness, adjust workflows
