Every church needs a clear pathway for discipleship—a system that moves people from wherever they are toward deeper engagement and spiritual maturity. The **Ministry Funnel** provides this pathway, guiding people from their first visit through to active service.

---

## What is the Ministry Funnel?

The Ministry Funnel is a visual framework showing how people progress through increasing levels of engagement with the church. Like any funnel, it's widest at the top (easiest entry) and narrows as commitment deepens.

```
       ┌─────────────────────┐
       │      WORSHIP        │  ← Entry Point (widest)
       │  Corporate Gatherings │
       └──────────┬──────────┘
                  ↓
          ┌──────────────┐
          │     WALK     │  ← Deeper Connection
          │  Small Groups │
          └──────┬───────┘
                 ↓
            ┌─────────┐
            │  WORK   │  ← Active Service (narrowest)
            │ Ministry │
            │  Teams   │
            └─────────┘
```

<Callout type="insight">
  The funnel isn't about hierarchy or status. It's about depth of engagement. Someone serving in ministry (Work) should still be worshiping and walking in community—they've simply added another dimension to their discipleship.
</Callout>

---

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: WORSHIP — The Entry Point

**Corporate worship gatherings are where most people first encounter the church.**

Sunday services serve as the wide opening of the funnel. They're:
- Accessible to anyone
- Low commitment to attend
- The primary first impression
- Where vision and values are communicated

**Goals at this stage:**
- Welcome guests warmly
- Provide meaningful worship experiences
- Communicate vision clearly
- Create easy next steps

| Metric | What to Track |
|--------|---------------|
| Attendance | How many people are showing up? |
| Guest retention | Are first-time guests returning? |
| Connection | Are people taking next steps? |

<Callout type="tip">
  Don't skip the funnel. Some churches try to get people serving immediately. But without the foundation of community (Walk), service becomes unsustainable. Let people progress naturally through the stages.
</Callout>

---

### Stage 2: WALK — Deeper Connection

**Small groups are where acquaintances become family.**

Corporate worship gathers the crowd, but small groups build the community. This is where people:
- Build authentic relationships
- Study Scripture together
- Share life's joys and struggles
- Experience care and accountability
- Develop spiritual friendships

**Goals at this stage:**
- Connect every regular attender to a small group
- Foster genuine relationships, not just meetings
- Create environments where people are known and know others
- Develop leaders within groups

| Metric | What to Track |
|--------|---------------|
| Small group participation | What percentage of attenders are in groups? |
| Group health | Are groups growing spiritually and relationally? |
| Leader development | Are new leaders emerging from groups? |

**The critical transition:**

Moving from Worship to Walk is the most important transition in the funnel. People who attend services but never connect in community:
- Remain anonymous
- Feel like spectators
- Are highly likely to drift away
- Never develop lasting commitment

<Callout type="important">
  Churches don't have a back door problem—they have a front door to side door problem. People leave because they never connected relationally. Small groups solve this.
</Callout>

---

### Stage 3: WORK — Active Service

**Ministry teams are where consumers become contributors.**

As people grow in worship and community, they're ready to serve. Work encompasses:
- Serving on ministry teams
- Using spiritual gifts for the body
- Taking ownership of the church's mission
- Investing in others' growth

**Goals at this stage:**
- Help people discover their gifts
- Place people in meaningful service roles
- Develop increasing responsibility over time
- Cultivate future leaders

| Metric | What to Track |
|--------|---------------|
| Volunteer engagement | What percentage of attenders are serving? |
| Team health | Are teams functioning well? |
| Leadership pipeline | Are servants becoming leaders? |

**Service roles include:**
- Facilities (setup, teardown, parking)
- Hospitality (greeting, guest services)
- Children's ministry
- Worship and production
- Small group leadership
- Assimilation
- Administration
- Prayer teams

## Why the Funnel Matters for Church Planting

### It Guides Your Priorities

You can't build everything at once. The funnel tells you what to focus on and when:

| Phase | Priority |
|-------|----------|
| **Core Group** | Establish Walk (small group community first) |
| **Launch** | Execute Worship (compelling corporate gatherings) |
| **Post-Launch** | Develop Work (ministry team engagement) |

### It Shapes Your Metrics

Instead of just counting attendance, you measure health at each stage:
- Are people worshiping? (attendance, engagement)
- Are people walking? (small group connection)
- Are people working? (service involvement)

A church with strong attendance but weak small groups will struggle to retain people. A church with active servers but no community will burn people out.

### It Informs Your Assimilation Strategy

The funnel shows you where to focus your assimilation efforts:
- Guest follow-up moves people from "visitor" to "attender" (Worship)
- Small group connection moves people from "attender" to "member" (Walk)
- Service opportunities move people from "member" to "minister" (Work)

## Common Funnel Failures

### Failure 1: No Clear Pathway

**Symptom:** People attend for months but never connect deeper.

**Solution:** Create obvious, easy next steps at each stage. Don't assume people know what to do next—tell them explicitly and make it simple.

### Failure 2: Pushing Too Fast

**Symptom:** New attenders are pressured to serve immediately; they burn out or leave.

**Solution:** Let people progress naturally. Build relationship before requesting commitment. Service should be the overflow of connection, not the price of entry.

### Failure 3: Leaking Funnel

**Symptom:** People move through stages but fall out along the way.

**Solution:** Track transitions. Who attended last month but not this month? Who visited a small group but didn't return? Follow up intentionally at each stage.

### Failure 4: Stuck at One Stage

**Symptom:** People plateau—they worship but never join a group, or they're in a group but never serve.

**Solution:** Regularly cast vision for the next stage. Create easy on-ramps. Address barriers honestly.

<Callout type="warning">
  A full sanctuary with empty small groups is a church waiting to decline. Numbers at the top of the funnel mean nothing if people aren't progressing through it.
</Callout>

---

## Implementing the Funnel from Day One

Even before launch, you can establish the funnel:

**During Core Group phase:**
- Your Core Group is already at Walk (small group community)
- Add Work by assigning responsibilities
- Vision Meetings function as proto-Worship gatherings

**At launch:**
- Worship gatherings become your entry point
- Small groups are ready to receive new people
- Ministry teams are staffed and functioning

**Post-launch:**
- Track movement through each stage
- Remove barriers to progression
- Celebrate stories of people moving deeper
