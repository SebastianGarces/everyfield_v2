You can run the best Vision Meeting in the world, but if you don't follow up effectively, it's wasted effort. Follow-up is the bridge between interest and commitment—the critical link that turns Vision Meeting attendees into Core Group members.

The importance of timely, effective follow-up cannot be overstated. Do this well, and the Core Group grows dramatically. Do it poorly, and you waste all the time you invested in planning Vision Meetings.

---

## The Follow-Up Problem

Most church planters underestimate follow-up. They pour energy into Vision Meetings—the presentation, the logistics, the invitation strategy—and then let follow-up slide.

Here's what typically happens:

1. Someone attends a Vision Meeting and expresses interest
2. Days pass without meaningful contact
3. Life gets busy, the interest fades
4. They never come back, never commit

**The window of opportunity is small.** Interest is highest immediately after the Vision Meeting. Every day without follow-up, that interest decreases.

<Callout type="important">
  Follow-up within 48 hours is essential. Wait longer, and you're swimming upstream against fading interest and competing priorities.
</Callout>

---

## What Great Follow-Up Accomplishes

When done well, follow-up accomplishes four critical objectives:

### 1. Answers Questions

Many people have legitimate questions that didn't come up during Q&A or that arose afterward. They need answers before making an informed decision.

Great follow-up provides those answers through personal conversation, not just email or printed materials. It creates space for people to voice concerns and get real responses.

### 2. Re-Casts the Vision

A follow-up meeting gives you opportunity to re-cast the vision in a personal, intimate setting. Many people need to hear something multiple times before it fully lands.

Great leaders continually cast vision of future days, events, and fruitfulness. Follow-up is one more opportunity to do that.

### 3. Interviews for Fit

Not everyone who attends and expresses interest is a good fit for the Core Group. Follow-up gives you the opportunity to assess fit before someone commits.

This protects both the person and the group—ensuring the right people end up in the right place.

### 4. Challenges to Commitment

Follow-up is where you personally challenge people to join the Core Group. This direct, personal invitation is often the difference between "interested" and "committed."

Some people are waiting to be asked. They need someone to look them in the eye and say, "We want you. Will you join us?"

---

## The Cost of Poor Follow-Up

When follow-up is neglected or poorly executed, the consequences are significant:

| What Happens | The Cost |
|--------------|----------|
| Questions go unanswered | People don't commit because concerns weren't addressed |
| Vision fades | Interest decreases; other priorities take over |
| Wrong people join | Without interviews, people who aren't a fit slip through |
| Commitment stays low | Without direct challenge, people stay on the fence |
| Momentum stalls | Core Group growth plateaus |

<Callout type="warning">
  Every Vision Meeting attendee who doesn't receive follow-up is a missed opportunity. If you're not going to follow up, you might as well not hold the meeting.
</Callout>

---

## The Follow-Up Mindset

Great follow-up requires a specific mindset:

### It's Not Optional

Follow-up isn't something you do when you have time. It's essential to the process. Build it into your rhythm as a non-negotiable.

### It's Personal

Mass emails don't count. Real follow-up requires personal conversation—phone calls, coffee meetings, face-to-face connection.

### It's Timely

The window is small. Within 48 hours is the standard. Within 24 hours is better.

### It's Persistent

Some people need multiple touchpoints before they commit. Don't give up after one attempt. Follow up, then follow up again, then follow up again.

### It's Honest

Follow-up isn't about selling people on something. It's about honest conversation to determine fit and make a genuine invitation.

---

## Who Owns Follow-Up?

In the early stages, the Senior Pastor should personally follow up with Vision Meeting attendees. As the Core Group grows, this responsibility can be shared:

### Senior Pastor

- Personal follow-up with high-interest individuals
- Challenging people to join the Core Group
- Final conversations before commitment

### Core Group Members

- Following up with people they personally invited
- Answering basic questions
- Building relationships that support follow-up conversations

<Callout type="tip">
  The person who invited someone is often the best person to follow up with them. They have existing relationship and credibility.
</Callout>

---

## The Follow-Up Process

A systematic follow-up process ensures no one falls through the cracks:

### Within 24-48 Hours

1. **Thank-you communication** — Email or text thanking them for attending
2. **Database entry** — Contact information added to your system
3. **Categorization** — High interest, moderate interest, low interest

### Within One Week

1. **Personal phone call** — Every attendee gets a call
2. **One-on-one meeting** — Scheduled with high-interest individuals
3. **Assignment** — If Core Group member invited them, they're assigned follow-up

### Ongoing

1. **Continued cultivation** — Regular touchpoints with interested individuals
2. **Invitation to next meeting** — Whether Vision Meeting or Core Group gathering
3. **Commitment conversation** — When appropriate, the direct ask

---

## Measuring Follow-Up Effectiveness

Track these metrics to evaluate your follow-up:

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| **% followed up within 48 hours** | Are you being timely? |
| **% converted from attendee to Core Group** | Is follow-up producing results? |
| **Average touchpoints before commitment** | How much cultivation is needed? |
| **Reasons for not committing** | What objections need addressing? |
