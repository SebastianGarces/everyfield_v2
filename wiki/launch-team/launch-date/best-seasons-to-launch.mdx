Not all launch dates are created equal. The time of year you choose to launch significantly impacts your initial attendance, community receptivity, and early momentum. Understanding seasonal patterns helps you set your church up for the strongest possible start.

---

## The Two Prime Launch Windows

Experience across hundreds of church plants has identified two optimal seasons for launching:

### 1. Fall Launch (Best Option)

**Target: As schools open (late August through mid-September)**

Fall is widely considered the best time to launch a church. Here's why:

| Factor | Why Fall Works |
|--------|---------------|
| **Family rhythms** | Families establish new routines as school starts—adding church fits naturally |
| **Summer reset** | People return from vacations ready for new commitments |
| **Full calendar** | You have September through May before summer slowdown |
| **Holiday runway** | Thanksgiving and Christmas services can build momentum |
| **Volunteer availability** | People are back from summer travel and ready to serve |

<Callout type="insight">
  Fall launches benefit from what psychologists call "fresh start effect"—people are more receptive to new habits and commitments at natural transition points like the start of a school year.
</Callout>

**Ideal Fall Launch Timing:**

- **Late August** — Capture families right as school begins
- **Early September** — Allow families to settle into school routines first
- **Mid-September** — Latest window before fall rhythms are fully established

Avoid launching after late September—by then, families have typically committed their Sundays elsewhere.

### 2. Spring Launch (Second Best Option)

**Target: 4-6 weeks before Easter**

Spring offers a secondary launch window with its own advantages:

| Factor | Why Spring Works |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Easter momentum** | The biggest attendance Sunday of the year is weeks away |
| **Visitor-ready season** | People are more likely to try a new church before Easter |
| **Weather improving** | Longer days and better weather increase mobility |
| **Energy renewal** | Post-winter enthusiasm creates receptivity |
| **Natural invite opportunity** | Easter provides a built-in reason to invite friends |

<Callout type="tip">
  If launching in spring, plan your Easter service as if it's your second launch Sunday. Many guests who visit on Easter will become regulars.
</Callout>

**Ideal Spring Launch Timing:**

- **Late January/Early February** — Maximum runway before Easter
- **Mid-February** — Allows 6-8 weeks of pre-Easter services
- **Early March** — Minimum time to establish rhythm before Easter surge

Avoid launching the week before Easter—you won't have time to establish any momentum before your biggest Sunday.

## Seasons to Avoid

### Summer (June-August)

Summer is generally the worst time to launch:

- Families are traveling and schedules are unpredictable
- Volunteer availability is inconsistent
- Attendance patterns are erratic
- You'll fight an uphill battle for momentum
- Three months of low attendance before fall pickup

<Callout type="warning">
  Summer launches often struggle to build momentum and can create discouragement in the Launch Team before you've really gotten started.
</Callout>

### December

While Christmas Eve services draw crowds, launching in December creates challenges:

- Holiday busyness limits people's bandwidth for new commitments
- Multiple weeks of irregular attendance (school breaks, travel)
- January attendance drop after holiday visitors
- Financial strain as people prioritize holiday spending

### Holiday Weekends (Any Season)

Avoid launching on weekends that compete with other activities:

- Memorial Day weekend
- Labor Day weekend
- July 4th weekend
- Super Bowl Sunday
- Major local events (big games, festivals, community celebrations)

## Regional Considerations

The "best" launch window varies somewhat by region:

### Northern Climates

- Fall launches work especially well
- Avoid deep winter launches when weather deters attendance
- Spring launches may need to account for unpredictable weather

### Southern Climates

- Fall window may extend into October
- Spring launches benefit from pleasant weather
- Summer remains challenging despite milder weather

### College Towns

- Consider academic calendars
- Fall launches align with students returning
- Avoid finals weeks and breaks

### Resort/Vacation Communities

- Summer may actually be peak season
- Work with local patterns rather than against them
- Consult with locals about community rhythms

## Planning Backward from Your Target Season

Once you identify your target launch window, work backward to ensure adequate preparation:

### For a Fall Launch

| Months Out | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **9-12 months** | Set launch date, begin leadership development |
| **6-9 months** | Intensify Core Group growth, identify key leaders |
| **4-6 months** | Transition to Launch Team, begin training |
| **2-4 months** | Equipment procurement, facility finalization |
| **4-6 weeks** | Pre-launch services, promotion execution |
| **2-3 weeks** | Final preparations, prayer intensification |

### For a Spring Launch

| Months Out | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **6-9 months** | Set launch date, begin leadership development |
| **4-6 months** | Intensify Core Group growth, identify key leaders |
| **3-4 months** | Transition to Launch Team, begin training |
| **2-3 months** | Equipment procurement, facility finalization |
| **4-6 weeks** | Pre-launch services, promotion execution |
| **2-3 weeks** | Final preparations, prayer intensification |

## What If Your Timeline Doesn't Fit?

Sometimes circumstances don't align with the ideal launch windows. If you're ready to launch but the timing isn't perfect, consider:

### Soft Launch Strategy

Launch internally with your Launch Team only, then do a public "Grand Opening" during a prime window:

- Internal launch in off-season (summer or late fall)
- Work out systems and processes with friendly audience
- Build momentum and confidence
- Public Grand Opening in fall or spring

### Extended Core Group Phase

If you're not ready for a prime window, continue building rather than rushing:

- Use the extra months for deeper training
- Grow your Core Group toward the higher end of targets
- Develop systems more thoroughly
- Enter the prime window with greater strength

<Callout type="important">
  It's better to wait for a prime launch window than to launch in an off-season just because you feel ready. The seasonal boost of a fall or spring launch is significant.
</Callout>
