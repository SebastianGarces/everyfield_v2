## 1. Annual Church Planting Volume

### Total U.S. Church Plants Per Year

- **Approximately 3,800-4,000 new Protestant churches are planted annually** in the United States, per the Lifeway Research / Leadership Network "State of Church Planting USA" study. This was described as "an all-time high" when first measured.
  - Source: [Study Reveals State of U.S. Church Planting - Christian Post](https://www.christianpost.com/news/study-reveals-state-of-u-s-church-planting-30104)
- In 2024 specifically, approximately **3,800 new Protestant churches launched**, while approximately **4,000 congregations closed**, resulting in a net loss of about 200 churches. This gap has narrowed significantly from a net loss of ~1,500 in 2019.
  - Source: [More Protestant churches closed than opened in the US in 2024 - Christian Today](https://www.christiantoday.com/news/more-protestant-churches-closed-than-opened-in-the-us-in-2024-new-analysis-finds)

### Southern Baptist Convention / NAMB (Send Network)

- **767 church plants in 2024** (the "Class of 2024"), including 83 replants.
- This was an **18% increase over 2023** and marked **four consecutive years of at least 600 new churches** planted by Southern Baptists.
- Total new SBC congregations in 2024: **964** (767 plants + 138 affiliations + 59 campuses).
- **217 newly endorsed church planters** at Send Network Orientation in 2025.
- **250,000 baptisms in 2024** -- the highest since 2017.
- Annie Armstrong Easter Offering: **over $70 million raised** for the third consecutive year.
  - Source: [NAMB Year in Review 2025 - Baptist Press](https://www.baptistpress.com/resource-library/news/namb-year-in-review-continued-growth-in-baptisms-church-planting-highlights-of-2025/)
  - Source: [Class of 2024 - Send Network](https://www.namb.net/send-network/resource/church-planting-north-america-2024/)

### ARC (Association of Related Churches)

- **35 new churches planted in 2024**, bringing the global total to **1,132 ARC churches**.
- Launch day attendance across new churches: **13,781** (up 27% year-over-year and 84% above the all-time average).
- **890 new converts** on launch days alone.
- Launch application interest **increased 72%** year-over-year.
- **91 couples** actively preparing to launch new ARC churches in 2025.
- In 2025, ARC planted **55 new churches** across 22 U.S. states and 8 countries, bringing the all-time total to **1,187 churches**.
  - Source: [ARC Highlights Church Planting Milestones in 2024 - GlobeNewsWire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/01/17/3011454/0/en/The-Association-of-Related-Churches-Highlights-Church-Planting-Milestones-in-2024.html)

### Acts 29

- Growth slowed significantly: 45% growth from 2012-2017 (195 churches added).
- In 2020: planted 25 new churches, with 70 existing churches joining the network.

## 2. Survival and Failure Rates

### The "80% Failure" Myth

The oft-cited statistic that "80% of church plants fail" has **no known original source** and is widely regarded as inaccurate by researchers. Actual data tells a more nuanced story.

- Source: [On Failed Church Plants - Lane Corley](https://lanecorley.com/2016/01/21/on-failed-church-plants-how-many-are-there-and-why/)

### NAMB Study: Year-by-Year Survival Rates

A North American Mission Board study (2007) tracked church plant survival over time:


| Year   | Survival Rate |
| ------ | ------------- |
| Year 1 | 99%           |
| Year 2 | 92%           |
| Year 3 | 81%           |
| Year 4 | 68%           |


- Source: [9 Myths About Church Planting - EFC](https://efcmaym.org/ministries/church-planting/9-myths-about-church-planting/)
- Source: [Equipping Church Planters for Success - Assemblies of God](https://enrichmentjournal.ag.org/Issues/2009/Fall-2009/Equipping-Church-Planters-for-Success)

### Lifeway / Leadership Network Finding

- **68% of the roughly 4,000 churches planted each year are still functioning four years later.** These churches may not yet be self-sufficient, but they are alive and many are thriving.
  - Source: [Study Reveals State of U.S. Church Planting - Christian Post](https://www.christianpost.com/news/study-reveals-state-of-u-s-church-planting-30104)

### Evangelical Covenant Church

- **86% survival rate after four years** for "well-conceived projects" that utilized training and coaching.
  - Source: [9 Myths About Church Planting - EFC](https://efcmaym.org/ministries/church-planting/9-myths-about-church-planting/)

### ARC Survival Rates

- ARC reports a **90% survival rate at the five-year mark** for churches within their network.
  - Source: [ARC Church Planting](https://www.arcchurches.com/equip/planting/)

### Broader Estimates

- Studies provide a wide range of **30-70% failure rates**, depending on methodology, timeframe, and definition of "failure." The low end (30% failure = 70% survival) is still significant: three out of every ten newly planted churches will not survive.
  - Source: [How Many Church Plants Fail? - Movements.net](https://www.movements.net/blog/tag/How+many+church+plants+fail%3F)

### Key Insight

**Support dramatically improves survival.** The gap between unsupported plants (~~68% survival at 4 years) and well-supported/assessed plants (~~86-90% survival at 4-5 years) is substantial. This is a core value proposition for a platform like EveryField.

## 3. Predictors of Success vs. Failure

### Ridley's 13 Competencies (The Gold Standard)

Dr. Charles Ridley (Indiana University, later Fuller Seminary) conducted the foundational study identifying **13 behavioral characteristics** of effective church planters. These remain the most widely used assessment framework:

1. **Visioning capacity**
2. **Intrinsically motivated**
3. **Creates ownership of ministry**
4. **Relates to the unchurched**
5. **Spousal cooperation**
6. **Effectively builds relationships**
7. **Commitment to church growth**
8. **Responsive to the community**
9. **Utilizes giftedness of others**
10. **Flexible and adaptable**
11. **Builds group cohesiveness**
12. **Resilience**
13. **Exercises faith**

The first six are considered **"knock-out factors"** -- competencies that cannot be taught and are absolute must-haves.

**Key finding:** In 2003, a study discovered that assessed church planters **substantially outperformed** non-assessed planters, with **at least 20% higher attendance each year** during the first 4 years. By year four, assessed planters averaged **25 conversions annually** vs. **12 for non-assessed planters**.

- Source: [Church Planter Assessment, Ridley's 13 Characteristics - ChurchPlanting.com](https://churchplanting.com/church-planter-assessment-ridleys-13-characteristics-and-test/)
- Source: [Thirteen Characteristics of Successful Planters - Minnesota UMC](https://www.minnesotaumc.org/thirteencharacteristicsofsuccessfulplanters)

### Lifeway Research: Factors Correlated with Success

1. **Meeting location:** Churches meeting in schools have significantly higher worship attendance, more first-time commitments to Christ, and are more likely to become financially self-sufficient.
2. **Community engagement:** Churches offering sports leagues, social gatherings, and children's events are more likely to attract previously unchurched people.
3. **Multiplication mindset:** New churches that launch at least one additional church within the first five years report higher attendance and more conversions.
4. **Adequate planter compensation:** Proper salary and health insurance are linked to higher worship attendance and greater likelihood of financial independence.
5. **Sponsoring/mother church:** Having a sending church is often crucial -- fewer early-stage burdens correlate with higher likelihood of success.
6. **Full-time pastor at launch:** Churches with full-time pastors at launch experience more growth than those with bivocational planters.
7. **Higher launch funding:** Research shows "more funding for a church plant's launch translates to larger attendance and quicker financial independence."
8. **Multiplication expectation:** "Church planters who didn't expect multiplication saw 0.3% growth; those expecting 10x growth saw 10% growth."
  - Source: [Lifeway Research Church Planter Assessment Tool](https://research.lifeway.com/2011/04/28/lifeway-research-unveils-innovative-church-planter-assessment-tool/)
  - Source: [New Churches More Effective at Reaching Unchurched - Lifeway](https://research.lifeway.com/2015/12/08/new-churches-more-effective-at-reaching-unchurched/)
  - Source: [In Church Planting, More Money Means More People - Christianity Today](https://www.christianitytoday.com/2023/01/church-planting-costs-startup-money-metrics/)
  - Source: [Important Trends Among Church Plants - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2022/09/30/important-trends-among-church-plants-and-multisite-campuses/)

### Assessment Impact

The Lifeway Research **Church Planter Candidate Assessment (CPCA)** was developed through interviews with 30+ assessment experts, tested across 500+ church planters, and contains 20+ statistically reliable scales. The best predictor of future success is **not discussing beliefs or future plans, but past experiences, practices, and habits.**

- Source: [Lifeway Research CPCA](https://research.lifeway.com/2011/04/28/lifeway-research-unveils-innovative-church-planter-assessment-tool/)

### Exponential's Multiplication Framework

Exponential identified five levels of church culture:


| Level   | Description                         | % of U.S. Churches |
| ------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Level 1 | Subtraction/Survival                | Combined: 93-96%   |
| Level 2 | Plateau/Survival                    |                    |
| Level 3 | Growing by Addition                 |                    |
| Level 4 | Reproducing                         | ~4-7%              |
| Level 5 | Multiplying (releasing and sending) | <1%                |


Exponential's goal: Move from 4-7% reproducing churches to a tipping point of **10-16%** (~53,000 congregations).

- Source: [Becoming Five - Exponential](https://exponential.org/product/becoming-five-multiplication-study/)

## 5. Financial Landscape

Costs vary enormously based on model, location, and ambition:


| Church Size Target            | Average Startup Costs | Average First-Year Costs |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------ |
| High-growth (200+ in 5 yrs)   | $100,000              | $225,000                 |
| Mid-size (100-200 attendance) | $84,500               | --                       |
| Small (under 100)             | $10,000               | $60,000                  |


**Cost scaling by eventual attendance:**


| Current Attendance | Average Launch Cost |
| ------------------ | ------------------- |
| 500+ people        | $322,000            |
| 201-500 people     | $200,000            |
| 101-200 people     | $141,000            |
| 51-100 people      | $100,000            |
| 50 or fewer        | $70,000             |


- Source: [In Church Planting, More Money Means More People - Christianity Today](https://www.christianitytoday.com/2023/01/church-planting-costs-startup-money-metrics/)

### Key Cost Categories

- **Staffing:** ~50% of a church budget. Multi-staff plants can exceed $150,000 for staffing alone.
- **Facilities:** Sunday rental ranges from $12,000 to $50,000/year.
- **Marketing/Outreach:** $0 (house church) to $80,000+.
- **United Methodist benchmark:** $300,000-$500,000 in denominational subsidy per plant.
  - Source: [How Much Should It Cost to Plant a New Church? - Lewis Center](https://www.churchleadership.com/focus/how-much-should-it-cost-to-plant-a-new-church/)

### Network Funding Amounts

- **ARC:** Pre-launch funding increased from **$50,000 to $100,000** per new church plant (2024). 82% of ARC's spending goes directly to launching, connecting, and equipping churches.
- **Acts 29:** Offers planters **$50,000** each.
- **NAMB/Send Network:** Funded through the Annie Armstrong Easter Offering ($70M+/year) and Cooperative Program giving.

### Funding Sources (Lifeway Research Data)

How church plants are funded at launch:


| Source                               | Percentage |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------- |
| Core group / launch team             | 26%        |
| Primary leader's personal investment | 15%        |
| Other local churches                 | 19%        |
| Denominations/networks/ministries    | 19%        |
| Leader's relatives                   | 6%         |
| Auxiliary sources                    | 3%         |
| Government grants                    | <1%        |
| Other                                | 12%        |


- Source: [Important Trends Among Church Plants - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2022/09/30/important-trends-among-church-plants-and-multisite-campuses/)

### Funding Timeline

- **3 years** is the low end for external funding support.
- **5 years** is the high end. If a planter still needs external funding after year 5, something may be seriously wrong.
- Reaching self-sustainability **before year 3** is considered very impressive.
  - Source: [Church Planting Tutorial: Finances - Passion for Planting](https://church-planting.net/church-planting-tutorial-finances/)

### Financial Stress on Planters (Barna Research)

- **60% of church planters** live on household income below the national average.
- **39%** earn between $35,000-$50,000 annually.
- **21%** earn less than $35,000/year.
- **32% describe themselves as financially struggling or surviving** (vs. 20% of general U.S. population).
- Only **23% feel financially secure** (vs. 41% of all adults).
- **33% have considered quitting ministry** due to financial strain.
- **47% in lower income brackets** have considered leaving ministry.
- **35% report marital strain** from financial stress.
- **69% of church planters were bivocational** during the first two years.
  - Source: [Church Planters and the Cost of Starting a Church - Barna](https://www.barna.com/research/church-planters-and-the-cost-of-starting-a-church/)

## 6. The Pipeline Problem

### The Core Issue

The No. 1 concern heard repeatedly from church leaders: **"Our pipeline of potential church planters has dried up."** Most church planting networks and denominations are struggling to find qualified planters.

- Source: [Why Has It Become Harder to Stock the Church Planter Pipeline? - Send Institute](https://www.sendinstitute.org/become-harder-stock-church-planter-pipeline/)
- Source: [Where Have All the Church Planters Gone? - Send Network](https://www.namb.net/send-network/resource/where-have-all-the-church-planters-gone/)

### NAMB's "More Money Than Men" Problem

NAMB has more financial resources than qualified planters to spend them on. If the convention demands more churches but the pipeline of genuinely qualified planters is limited, NAMB faces a genuine dilemma.

- Source: [More Money Than Men: The NAMB Church Planting Problem - Christ Over All](https://christoverall.com/article/concise/more-money-than-men-the-namb-church-planting-problem/)

### Why the Pipeline is Shrinking

1. **The youth pastor pipeline has dried up:** Previously, youth pastors and associate staff with 5-6 years of experience naturally transitioned into church planting. This pathway has weakened.
2. **Gen Z reluctance:** Young adults won't settle for low-paying church planting jobs and many have been burned by the church.
3. **Longer development timelines:** "Seasoned youth pastor into effective church planter is a 2-year project; doing the same with a young seminary graduate is more like a 10-year project."
4. **Early adopter pool exhausted:** Networks that initially attracted ready-made planters have "fished out the pool" without adequately stocking it.
5. **Aging planters:** The median age of a church planter at launch has risen from **36 (in 2007) to 42 (current)**, indicating fewer young leaders entering the pipeline.
  - Source: [The Pipeline is Shrinking - Bob Thune](https://www.bobthune.com/blog/2017/06/pipeline-shrinking)
  - Source: [Important Trends Among Church Plants - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2022/09/30/important-trends-among-church-plants-and-multisite-campuses/)

### Only 4-7% of Churches Are Reproducing

Exponential's research shows that **93-96% of U.S. churches are NOT reproducing** -- they exist at Levels 1-3 (subtraction, plateau, or addition growth only). This means the vast majority of existing churches are not generating new planters.

- Source: [A Movement of Multiplication - Exponential](https://exponential.org/a-movement-of-multiplication/)

### Proposed Solutions

- **Shift from recruitment to development:** Churches must move from looking for ready-made planters to developing missional leaders internally.
- **"Just Ask" strategy:** Research suggests many potential planters have never been asked. Simply asking qualified leaders to consider planting has an outsized impact.
  - Source: [Just Ask: A Simple Solution to the Church Planter Shortage - Outreach Magazine](https://outreachmagazine.com/features/82927-just-ask-a-simple-solution-to-the-church-planter-shortage.html)
- **Extended preparation:** Encouraging young leaders to serve 5-10 years in ministry before planting.
- **Pipeline systems:** Building internal church planter pipelines within existing congregations.
  - Source: [Internal Church Planter Pipeline - Exponential](https://exponential.org/internal-church-planter-pipeline/)

## 7. Broader Market Context

### Church Closures vs. New Plants


| Year  | New Churches | Churches Closed | Net Change |
| ----- | ------------ | --------------- | ---------- |
| ~2014 | ~3,000       | ~4,500          | -1,500     |
| 2019  | ~3,500       | ~5,000          | -1,500     |
| 2024  | ~3,800       | ~4,000          | -200       |


The gap is narrowing, but closures still outpace new plants. Some estimates are more dire: Thom Rainer projects **up to 15,000 church closures in a single year** for the first time ever.

- Source: [More Protestant Churches Closed Than Opened in 2024 - Christian Today](https://www.christiantoday.com/news/more-protestant-churches-closed-than-opened-in-the-us-in-2024-new-analysis-finds)

### Churches Needed to Keep Pace

- To maintain the current ratio of 1 church per 1,000 people through 2050, the U.S. needs a **net gain of ~2,000 churches annually**.
- To actually grow the church-to-population ratio, the U.S. would need **8,000-11,000 new churches per year** -- 2-3x the current rate.
- This means an additional **76,000 new congregations** are needed between 2010 and 2050.
  - Source: [4 Trends Shaping Church Planting Through 2050 - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2021/03/30/4-trends-shaping-church-planting-and-growth-through-2050/)
  - Source: [The Great Opportunity - Starting More Churches](https://www.greatopportunity.org/starting-more-churches)

### Newer Churches Outperform

Within the SBC, **congregations founded since 2000 were the ONLY group to record overall membership growth** (12% over five years). All churches established before 2000 experienced declines.

- Source: [More Protestant Churches Closed Than Opened in 2024 - Christian Today](https://www.christiantoday.com/news/more-protestant-churches-closed-than-opened-in-the-us-in-2024-new-analysis-finds)

### Post-Pandemic Reshuffling

Half of U.S. Protestant pastors say their churches are growing, but this is driven by a "reshuffling" where Christians returned to church post-COVID but not necessarily to the same church. This period of easy growth may be ending.

- Source: [Half of Churches Experiencing Post-Pandemic Attendance Growth - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2025/03/18/half-of-churches-experiencing-post-pandemic-attendance-growth/)

### Demographic Shifts

- **Multiethnic planting is rising:** 60%+ of the newest SBC plants are led by non-Anglo planters. Nearly 20% of SBC congregations are now non-Anglo.
- **70% of new churches** locate in cities or dense inner suburbs.
- **U.S. projected to become majority-minority by 2045** (William Frey, Brookings), making diverse church planting essential.
  - Source: [4 Trends Shaping Church Planting Through 2050 - Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2021/03/30/4-trends-shaping-church-planting-and-growth-through-2050/)

### Church Management Software Market

- Market valued at **$247M-$886M** (2024-2025 estimates, depending on source), projected to reach **$393M-$518M by 2031-2032** at ~5.4-9.7% CAGR.
- Key trend: Cloud-based solutions, AI/ML integration for automation.
- Major players: Planning Center, Breeze ChMS, Church Community Builder, Tithe.ly, Pushpay.
- **No dominant player specifically focused on the church planting journey** (pre-launch through self-sustainability). Most ChMS tools are designed for established churches.
  - Source: [Church Management Software Market - Straits Research / GlobeNewsWire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/11/29/2787679/0/en/Global-Church-Management-Software-Market-Size-is-Estimated-to-Reach-USD-393-million-by-2031-Growing-at-a-CAGR-of-5-4-Straits-Research.html)

## 8. Key Takeaways for EveryField

### The Opportunity

1. **~4,000 new church plants per year** represents a consistent, renewable market. Each plant needs tools from pre-launch through maturity.
2. **The pipeline problem is a software problem in disguise.** Networks struggle to identify, assess, track, and develop planters. A platform that streamlines pipeline management addresses their No. 1 pain point.
3. **Support dramatically improves survival:** Assessed/coached plants survive at 86-90% vs. ~68% for unsupported ones. Tools that facilitate assessment, coaching, and milestone tracking have measurable impact on survival.
4. **Financial tracking is critical:** 33% of planters consider quitting due to financial strain. 69% are bivocational. Tools that help planters track budgets, funding timelines, and path to self-sustainability address an acute need.
5. **The market is underserved:** Existing ChMS tools (Planning Center, Breeze, etc.) are designed for established churches, not for the unique pre-launch-to-sustainability journey of a church plant.
6. **Networks are the distribution channel:** 45-60+ networks actively support planters. Building for network administrators (not just individual planters) creates a B2B2C distribution model.
7. **Multiplication is the aspiration:** Only 4-7% of churches reproduce. Tools that help churches build internal planter pipelines tap into the movement's most articulated goal.

### Market Sizing (Back-of-Envelope)

- ~4,000 new plants/year x average 3-5 year journey = **12,000-20,000 active church plants** at any given time
- 45-60 sending networks managing pipelines
- $247M-$886M existing ChMS market, with no dominant church-planting-specific player
- ARC alone increased pre-launch funding to $100K per plant -- these are not budget-constrained customers

### Critical Pain Points to Address


| Pain Point                        | Evidence                                                      | EveryField Opportunity                                            |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pipeline management               | "Our pipeline has dried up" -- No. 1 network concern          | Pipeline CRM, assessment tracking, development milestones         |
